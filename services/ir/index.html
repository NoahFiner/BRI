Interventional Services

Our services include the following as well as working with the physicians at the Boulder Community Health to provide support in as minimally invasive a procedure as possible.


▣ Diagnosing possible blood vessel issues through an X-ray exam of the arteries and veins . And where necessary, using a catheter to inject a contrast agent into the blood vessel. (angioplasty)


▣ Treating blood vessel abnormalities in the brain and elsewhere (arteriovenous malformations) without surgery by guiding thin catheters to the site and injecting a substance that blocks the supply of blood to the affected blood vessel. (arteriovenous malformations)

▣ Opening up blocked ducts to drain bile from the liver with a stent. (billary drainage)


▣ Opening blocked or narrowed blood vessels by inserting a small balloon into the vessel and inflating it. (balloon angioplasty).


▣ Locating areas of internal bleeding and injecting a clotting substance through a thin catheter to stop the bleeding.


▣ Inserting a tube beneath the skin and into the blood vessels to deliver medication or nutrients directly into the blood stream.


▣ Delivery of cancer-fighting agents to the site of a cancer tumor.


▣ Delivering clotting agents to a bleeding area or block blood flow to an aneurysm or a fibroid tumor in the uterus. (embolization)


▣ Inserting a feeding tube into the stomach where the patient is unable to take sufficient food by mouth.


▣ Opening blocked grafts to treat kidney failure through angioplasty or thrombolysis.


▣ Draining a persistent infection (abscess) in the body by inserting a catheter through the skin to the infection site.


▣ Diagnostic test for breast, lung and other cancers through a needle biopsy.


▣ Treating a variety of medical conditions, including narrowed vessel pathways, by inserting a small flexible tube made of plastic or wire mesh.


▣ Dissolving blood clots by injecting clot-busting drugs at the site of the clot. can be used to treat blood clots in the brain to reverse the effects of a stroke as well as vein thrombosis in the leg to prevent permanent disability.


▣ Treating urinary tract obstructions sometimes caused by kidney stones by inserting a catheter through the skin and into the blocked kidney to drain the urine.


▣ Uterine Fibroid Embolization to cut off the blood supply to the fibroid and causing them to shrink and die so that symptoms to subside (also known as uterine artery embolization).


▣ Varicose Vein Treatment
